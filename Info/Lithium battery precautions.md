When these batteries short circuit or suffer physical damage, they ignite rapidly and create a self-sustaining chain reaction where ***fuel, oxidizer, and heat*** are all provided by the battery itself. Most oxygen-cutting fire extinguishers won't work because the battery generates its own oxygen.

Since you cannot remove the fuel or oxidizer during combustion, the only option is to extract heat.

## Storage protocol:
- Store batteries at 30-50% charge (NOT fully discharged—this can damage cells and make them unstable)
- Cover terminals with electrical tape or terminal caps to prevent shorts
- Use fireproof LiPo bags for individual batteries to contain fire and blast if thermal runaway occurs
- Keep batteries away from public areas
- Keep water and dry sand immediately accessible (for small batteries)
- Inspect weekly for swelling or physical damage (discard batteries immediately if observed).
## If fire occurs:
1. Best option: Specialized lithium battery fire extinguisher (see list below)
2. Alternative (for small batteries like power bank etc): Submerge in large volume of water OR cover completely with dry sand
3. Evacuate immediately and call emergency services (fire department)
