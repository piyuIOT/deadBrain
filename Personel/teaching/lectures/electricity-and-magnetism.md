# Electricity & Magnetism (बिजली व चुम्बक)

**Objective:** Learn responsible use of electricity and teamwork. Use compass, verify scientific claims. Build circuits, intro to coding. Understand effects of electricity and daily-life applications.

> [!note] Inclusion Note
> In mixed-gender groups, actively ensure girls get hands-on roles in experiments — they are often excluded from such tasks by default.

## Activity 1 — Save Electricity, Build the Nation (40 min + HW)

### 1a — Homework: Survey
- Show video on why not to waste electricity (money savings, environmental protection).
- Explain **kWh** (unit of electricity consumption). Rate: ~Rs 2.50 per kWh.
- Divide into teams of 10. Each team surveys the school for 1 week:

| Device | Energy (kWh) | Cost (Rs) | Suggestion |
|--------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Class 6 fan | 8 | 20 | Turn off during recess; service the fan |
| | | | |

- Teams note: which device, how long used, kWh consumed, cost, and suggestions to reduce it.
- Subdivide team into sections covering different parts of the school. Only 1 student per team presents in Part 1b.

### 1b — Presentation (30 min)
- Each team: 1 student presents survey results in 3 minutes.
- Teacher reveals actual school electricity bill — compare with student estimates, discuss discrepancy.
- Compile suggestions into a chart → post on school office bulletin board.

## Activity 2 — Compass (60 min)

### 2a — Experiment (40 min)
**Materials:** Laptop, projector, many magnets

1. Ask: in which direction does the sun set? (Most say west.) How can you verify using science class knowledge?
2. Recall: rubbing a magnet on a needle magnetises it.
3. Give each student a magnet → make their own compass using available materials.
4. Take students outside at sunset → locate sunset direction using their compass.
5. Note: sunset direction varies by date; the annual average direction is west.

### 2b — Discussion (20 min)
- Discuss: how can classroom science knowledge be used to verify everyday claims?
- Ask students to discuss with family and find other claims they can test with school science.
- Encourage students to actually carry out those experiments.

## Activity 3 — Electric Circuit, Components, Conductors & Insulators (90 min)

### 3a — Build a Simple Circuit (20 min)
**Materials:** 9V battery + connector, wire, LED bulb, electrical tape, paper clip, switch

Steps:
1. Pre-place materials in each group's tray.
2. Ask: how to connect components to make the bulb glow? Give time to think.
3. Draw the circuit on blackboard.
4. Students cut wire, strip both ends, then connect:
   - One wire end → bulb → battery **(–)** terminal
   - Other wire end → battery **(+)** terminal
   - Free ends → switch
5. Result: closed circuit → bulb glows.

### 3b — Component Symbols (30 min)

## Activity 4 — Lalita's Tea (110 min)

### Storyline
Lalita lives in Rakheegarhi village. On a cold morning she wants tea but is too tired to pull water from the well. She has heard electricity can pump water at the press of a button. She needs your help.

### 4a — Electric Water Pump (50 min)
**Materials:** Battery, pipe, DC motor, plastic bag, tube

1. Show video → demonstrate building the pump.
2. Divide into teams of 4 → each team builds their own pump.
3. Test: how high can each team's pump shoot water?
4. Discussion: do they have such a pump at school or home?

### 4b — Hydrogen Gas (60 min)
**Story continues:** Lalita thanks the kids but finds her gas cylinder is empty. Solution: electricity can also produce hydrogen gas through electrolysis — which can be used as fuel.

1. Watch video 2 → demonstrate electrolysis setup (water → H₂ gas).
2. Teams build their own electrolysis device.
3. Final discussion: how can textbook science be applied to make real life easier?
