# Sound (ध्वनि)

**Objective:** Understand what sound is and its properties. Learn about mediums of sound propagation. Understand amplitude and frequency of vibrations. Learn about human voice and hearing.

## Activity 1 — Sound and Vibration (45 min)

**Materials:** Fork/spoon, speaker, cup, bowl, foil, scissors, tape, sprinkle colours

Sound is produced by vibrating objects. Key relationship: **vibration → sound, sound → vibration**.

**Experiments:**
- Strike a fork/tuning fork — feel vibration, hear sound.
- Play audio through a speaker — observe the surface vibrating.
- **Dancing Cup experiment:** Balloon membrane over a cup + sprinkle colours on top. Sound from speaker makes the balloon vibrate, particles dance.

**Discussion:** List sounds around us and identify the vibrating source for each.

## Activity 2 — Amplitude and Frequency (60 min)

**Materials:** Metal glass, tea spoon, thermocol ball, smartphone, musical instruments

### Amplitude (आयाम) — Loudness
- Amplitude determines **loudness (प्रबलता)** of sound.
- Higher amplitude → louder sound.
- Measured in **decibels (dB)**.

**Experiment:**
1. Tap a metal glass gently with a spoon → soft sound.
2. Tap hard → louder sound.
3. Suspend a small thermocol ball touching the rim of the glass. Strike the glass — observe how far the ball is displaced. Displacement = measure of amplitude.

### Frequency (आवृत्ति) — Pitch
- Frequency determines **pitch (तीक्षणता)** of sound.
- Higher frequency → higher/shriller pitch.
- Lower frequency → lower/deeper pitch.

**Hearing ranges of different animals:**

| Animal | Frequency Range |
|--------|----------------|
| Human (मानव) | 20 – 20,000 Hz |
| Dog (कुत्ता) | 200 Hz – 12 kHz |
| Cow (गाय) | 13 Hz – 35 kHz |
| Bat (चमगादड़) | 60 – 70 kHz |
| Elephant (हाथी) | 17 Hz – 10.5 kHz |
| Dog/Cat (कुत्ता/बिल्ली) | 16 Hz – 200 kHz |
| Bird (पक्षी) | 300 Hz – 7.9 kHz |
| Cricket (किरली) | 55 Hz – 7.7 kHz |

**Activity:** Listen to sounds of different frequencies, guess high/low. Verify with a **Sound Analyzer App** on smartphone. Groups of 3–5.

### Musical Instruments
- Observe instruments around you, play them, identify the vibrating part.
- Students can make their own instruments.

## Activity 3 — Sound and Medium (45 min)

**Materials:** Glass tumbler, mobile phone/bell, water bucket, metal metre scale, copy-pen

**Key question:** Does sound need a medium to travel?

### Experiment 1 — Air as medium
1. Place a remote-activated phone/bell inside a glass tumbler.
2. One student activates it from a distance; second student listens normally — sound heard clearly.
3. Second student seals lips around the glass opening and sucks air out while bell rings.
4. Observation: as air is removed, sound becomes **fainter**. Restore air → sound becomes loud again.
5. Conclusion: **denser medium → louder sound transmission**.

### Experiment 2 — Liquid medium
1. Fill a bucket with clean water.
2. Ring a small bell inside the water (don't touch the bucket walls).
3. Second student places ear carefully on the water surface.
4. Observation: bell sound is heard → **sound travels through liquids**.

### Experiment 3 — Solid medium
1. Hold one end of a metal metre scale to your ear.
2. Ask a friend to scratch/tap the other end gently.
3. Observation: scratching sound heard clearly through the scale, but people standing nearby cannot hear it.
4. Conclusion: **sound travels through solids**.

**Summary:** Sound requires a medium — it travels through solids, liquids, and gases. It **cannot travel in vacuum**.

## Activity 4 — Our Throat and Our Ears (45 min)

**Materials:** Laptop/computer, speaker, copy-pen, smartphone

### Human Voice Production
- **Lungs + vocal cords (वाक्-तंतु)** together produce vibrations → sound from mouth.

### Human Ear (कान)
- **Eardrum (कर्णपटह)** = stretched rubber sheet that vibrates with incoming sound waves.
- Reference video: *How the ear works (Hindi)* — Khan Academy / YouTube.

### Key Concepts

**Audible vs Inaudible sounds:**
- Humans hear: **20 Hz – 20,000 Hz**
- Below 20 Hz = **Infrasound (अवश्रव्य)**
- Above 20,000 Hz = **Ultrasound (पराश्रव्य)**

**Noise vs Music:**
- Make a list of sounds around you. Classify as: melodious / noisy / musical.
- Use **Sound Analyzer App** on smartphone to measure and compare.

**Sound Pollution (ध्वनि प्रदूषण):**
- Discuss: sources of noise pollution around us.
- Discuss: measures to control noise pollution.
- Reference documentary videos (provided in PDF links).
