## Activity 2 — Evaporation, Condensation & Rain (135 min)

**Materials:** Glass jars, lids, water, ice, foil, cellotape, vinegar, salt, mud, butter paper, a sunlit tree

Groups of 3–4. Half do evaporation, half do condensation.

### Evaporation Experiment
1. Fill 2 identical glass jars with equal water.
2. Cover one, leave the other open.
3. Place both in strong sunlight for 30–60 min.
4. Compare water levels — open jar loses more water → **evaporation**.

### Condensation Experiment
1. Fill a glass/jug with ice cubes, cover the top.
2. After some time, observe tiny water droplets on the **outer surface**.
3. Water vapour in air contacts the cold surface → condenses into liquid.
4. Observation table: *क्या देखा? — क्या महसूस किया? — क्या कारण हो सकता है?*

### Rain (Water Cycle) Demo
- Teacher demo: hot water + ice + container = miniature rain.
- Build water cycle diagram together: evaporation + condensation → rain.

### Transpiration
- Tie a plastic foil sheet around a leafy branch of a tree.
- Observe over 4 days — water droplets appear inside → **transpiration** from leaves.

**Key fact:** Earth's surface is ~75% water — but it is limited and cycles continuously.

**Composition of water on Earth (demonstration table):**

| Container | Represents |
|-----------|-----------|
| Bucket | All water on Earth |
| Mug | Freshwater fraction |
| Glass | Groundwater (usable) |
| Spoon | All rivers and lakes combined |

- Where could water have been saved or reused?
- **Key gender question:** Which water-related tasks does your brother NOT do but you do? Your father doesn't but your mother does? Why?

## Activity 6 — Water Conservation Methods & Experiments

Class divided into 5 groups. Each group works on one topic:

### Group 1 — Rainwater Harvesting / Groundwater Recharge
- Discuss: where do we extract groundwater? What machines? (Handpump, submersible pump)
- Ask a farmer: how deep is water now vs 10 years ago?
- Install groundwater recharge system at school (see video link in PDF).
- Case study: **Alwar** (video) + **Kutch — Rukmavati river** check dam story.

**Kutch Case Study:** In 1989, villagers built 18 check dams on the Rukmavati river. Groundwater rose → wells have water year-round → water that once flowed to sea now available for irrigation.

### Group 2 — Drip Irrigation / Micro Irrigation
- Build a simple drip irrigation model using: plastic bottle, straw, cellotape, pointed rod.
- Write procedure. Note if you've seen this used in your village.

### Group 3 — Wastewater Path (Sewage Mapping)

## Activity 10 — Is Air Everywhere? (60 min)

**Materials:** Empty bottle, bucket of water, candles, glass tumbler, lime water, test tubes, dark room with sunlit window

### Air occupies space
1. Take an empty bottle. Is it really empty?
2. Invert it and push vertically into a bucket of water → water doesn't enter (air blocks it).
3. Tilt the bottle → bubbles come out, water enters.
4. **Conclusion:** The "empty" bottle was full of air. Air occupies space.

### Air contains Oxygen
1. Place 2 equal candles burning on a table.
2. Cover one with an inverted glass tumbler.
3. Covered candle goes out after some time. Uncovered keeps burning.
4. **Conclusion:** Oxygen inside the glass was consumed. Air contains ~**21% Oxygen**.

### Air contains CO₂ — Lime Water Test
1. Mix 2–3 spoons of lime (चूना) in water the previous day.
2. Next day: fill a test tube with lime water.
3. Blow into it through a straw.
4. Water turns **milky** → CO₂ in exhaled air reacts with lime water.
5. **CO₂ in atmosphere** = 0.04% (confirms CO₂ is present).

### Air contains Nitrogen
- Nitrogen = **78%** of air. Non-reactive. Doesn't change litmus.
- Used in nitrogen fixation in crops (dal, soybean).

### Air contains Water Vapour
- Repeat the condensation experiment from Activity 2.

### Air contains Dust Particles
1. Darken a room completely. Open a tiny crack in the window.
2. Observe the sunbeam — tiny glowing particles spinning = **dust**.
3. Nose hairs + mucus filter dust before it enters lungs. → Never breathe through mouth.

**Air Composition Summary:**

| Gas | % |
|-----|---|
| Nitrogen (N₂) | ~78% |
| Oxygen (O₂) | ~21% |
| CO₂ + other gases + water vapour | ~1% |

## Activity 13 — Air Pollution in Village (45 min)

Discussion:
- Has bad air quality caused illness in your village? When? Note all instances.
- Sources of bad air in village?
- Has bad water quality caused illness? When?
- Sources of polluted water?

Introduce: **Air pollution** and **Water pollution** are caused by human activity. Examples: stubble burning (पराली) → serious respiratory diseases in winter.

**Why do farmers burn stubble?**
- Only 10–15 days between two crop cycles → no time for proper disposal.
- Mechanical harvesters leave 10–30 cm stubble — costly to clear by hand.
- No market for crop residue (straw used to make thatching but demand declined).

## Activity 14 — Air Pollution Experiments (90 min)

**Materials:** Aluminium foil, matches, ice, glass jar, tree leaves, eggshells, pH paper, vinegar, vaseline, tape, 3 thermometers, baking soda, 2 sealed glass jars, NCERT Class 8 Science book

Groups of 4–5. Each group does one experiment and presents to others.

### Experiment 1 — Smog Creation *(supervision required)*
- Read NCERT Class 8, Section 18.2.
- Key questions: What pollutants cause air pollution? Which sources exist in your village? What is smog?
- Create smog in a transparent jar (see video link in PDF).
- Record: what change appeared inside the jar?

### Experiment 2 — Acid Rain Effects *(supervision required)*
- Read NCERT Class 8, Section 18.3.
- What is acid rain? How does it form? What fuels reduce acid rain?
- Vehicle/industrial exhaust + water vapour → HNO₃ + H₂SO₄ → acid rain.
- Effect on Taj Mahal marble — "Marble Cancer".
- Simulate with vinegar on eggshells/leaves. Observe pH paper changes.

### Experiment 3 — Measuring Pollution in Different Locations
- Apply vaseline on paper strips. Place in 3–4 different locations for a fixed time.
- Compare dust/particle accumulation — see which location is most polluted.

### Experiment 4 — Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming
- Read NCERT Class 8, Section 18.4.
- Questions: What is greenhouse effect? Which gases are greenhouse gases? Why is it necessary for Earth? How is it causing global warming now?
- Experiment: two sealed glass jars — one with CO₂ (via baking soda + vinegar), one normal air. Place in sunlight. Record temperature rise over days.
