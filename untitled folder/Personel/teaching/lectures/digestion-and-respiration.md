# Digestion and Respiration in Animals (प्राणियों में पाचन और श्वसन क्रिया)

## Objectives
- Understand position, shape, and functions of different types of teeth
- Know healthy and unhealthy foods/habits for teeth
- Know functions of the alimentary canal and organs of the digestive system
- Understand concepts of inhalation, exhalation, and breathing rate
- Understand lung function and respiratory systems of living organisms
- Understand Air Quality Index (AQI) and harmful effects of smoke on lungs
- Understand effects of pollution and possible solutions

## Part 1: Digestion

### Activity 1: Run, Eat and Run
**Time:** 20 min | **Materials:** Chocolate, open running space

Ask children to run, then eat chocolate, then run again. Ask them which body part helped in each action. Conclude: teeth helped in eating, legs helped in running — both are important body parts.

---

### Activity 2: Let's Carve and Arrange (Types of Teeth)
**Time:** 30 min | **Materials:** Chalk, pink/white paper, carving tools

Divide into 4 groups. Each group gets 5–7 chalk pieces, pink paper, carving tools. Make models of 4 tooth types: **Incisor, Canine, Premolar, Molar**.

#### Tooth Types — Human Dentition

| Type | Hindi | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| Incisor (छेदक/कृंतक) | कृंतक | Cutting |
| Canine (भेदक/रदनक) | रदनक | Tearing |
| Premolar (अग्रचर्वणक) | अग्रचर्वणक | Chewing |
| Molar (चर्वणक) | चर्वणक | Grinding/Chewing |

#### Dental Formulas
- **Milk teeth (deciduous):** Formula 2102/2102 = 10 per jaw → **20 total**
  - Per quadrant: 4 incisors, 2 canines, 0 premolars, 4 molars
- **Permanent teeth:** Formula 2123/2123 → **32 total**
  - Per quadrant: 2 incisors, 1 canine, 2 premolars, 3 molars

> [!note] Non-obvious fact
> Humans get teeth twice in a lifetime. The 20 milk teeth are replaced by permanent teeth, so exactly 20 teeth appear twice in a lifetime.

**Discussion questions:**
- If we only had molars, could we eat all foods? → No — apples, carrots need incisors/canines to bite first
- What foods can canines help eat? → Apple, carrot

### Activity 4: Eating Upside Down
**Time:** 10 min | **Materials:** Any fruit or food

Give each group a fruit (banana, apple). Ask them to eat it in different positions:
- Sitting upright
- Lying flat
- Lying upside down

**Key question:** Why were they able to eat in any position?
**Answer:** The esophagus has muscles that push food toward the stomach via peristalsis — direction of body doesn't matter.

> [!warning] Safety note
> Ensure no fruit/food gets lodged in any child's airway during this activity.

---

### Activity 5: Press Ball Under Sock
**Time:** 15 min | **Materials:** Sock, tennis ball

Put a tennis ball inside a sock. Push it from one end to the other using hands.

**Analogy:** This mimics peristalsis — esophageal muscles push food toward the stomach the same way hands push the ball through the sock.

> [!warning] Safety
> Children should not do this without supervision.

### Activity 6: How the Digestive System Works
**Time:** 20 min | **Materials:** Video, projector, laptop, speaker

Ask children: how does the body digest food? Which organs are involved?

**Video:** https://youtu.be/CiTgITL8wDA?feature=shared

#### Digestive System Organs and Functions

```
Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Rectum
```

| Organ | Function |
|-------|----------|
| Mouth (मुँह) | Chewing; breaking food into smaller pieces |
| Esophagus (ग्रासनली) | Transporting food from mouth to stomach |
| Stomach (पेट) | Breaking complex food into simpler substances |
| Small Intestine (छोटी आंत) | Absorbing nutrients into blood cells for transport |
| Large Intestine (बड़ी आंत) | Converting undigested material into waste for excretion |

#### How Food is Digested in the Stomach
When food reaches the stomach, it releases three things:
1. **Hydrochloric acid (HCl)** — dissolves food
2. **Pepsin** — digests protein
3. **Mucus** — protects stomach lining from acid

#### 6 Main Functions of the Digestive System
1. Ingestion (अंतर्ग्रहण)
2. Motility (गतिशीलता)
3. Secretion (स्राव)
4. Digestion (पाचन)
5. Absorption (अवशोषण)
6. Excretion (उत्सर्जन)

## Part 2: Respiration

### Activity 12: Yoga / Exercise
**Time:** 10 min | **Materials:** Open space

Children sit in a circle, close eyes. Facilitator guides deep breathing — inhale, hold, exhale. Children feel chest expand on inhale and contract on exhale.

**Key concept:** One inhalation + one exhalation = one breath.

---

### Activity 13: Sit, Run and Walk (Breathing Rate)
**Time:** 20 min | **Materials:** Playground, paper, pen, stopwatch

Measure breathing rate by counting how many times the belly/chest rises in 1 minute.

Groups of 4–5, each with a stopwatch. Record breathing rates for each condition:

| | | | | |

### Activity 14: How Our Lungs Work (Model)
**Time:** 30 min | **Materials:** Plastic bottle, balloon, straw, tape, scissors

> [!warning] Safety
> Use scissors carefully.

Build a lung model. Facilitator prepares one in advance.

**Reference video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P_Cah94kYEs

Each group also draws a diagram of the respiratory system and writes the steps of model construction.

**Discussion questions:**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Who carries oxygen from lungs? | Left atrium receives oxygenated blood from lungs via pulmonary vein; right atrium receives deoxygenated blood from body via vena cava |
| How does oxygen enter the lungs? | Via trachea → bronchi → alveoli; alveoli are responsible for gas exchange |

### Activity 15: Exhale into Lime Water
**Time:** 10 min | **Materials:** Lime water (calcium hydroxide solution), test tube, straw

Pour lime water into test tube. Blow through straw into the water.

**Observation:** Lime water turns milky.

**Explanation:** CO₂ in exhaled air reacts with Ca(OH)₂:
$$\text{Ca(OH)}_2 + \text{CO}_2 \rightarrow \text{CaCO}_3 \downarrow + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

The white precipitate (CaCO₃) makes the water appear milky.

> [!warning] Safety
> Ensure lime water does not get sucked back into anyone's mouth through the straw.

### Activity 17: Air Quality Index (AQI)
**Time:** 30 min | **Materials:** AQI graphs of Indian cities

#### AQI Scale

| Colour | Level | Index Value | Effect |
|--------|-------|-------------|--------|
| Green | Good | 0–50 | Minimal or no risk |
| Yellow | Satisfactory | 51–100 | Air ok; minor problems for sensitive people |
| Orange | Moderately polluted | 101–150 | May affect sensitive groups |
| Red | Poor | 151–200 | Health risk for general public; serious for sensitive groups |
| Purple | Very poor | 201–300 | Dangerous for everyone |
| Maroon | Severe | 301+ | Health emergency |

> [!note]
> AQI = "One number – One colour – One description". 6 colour-coded categories.

**Class exercise (pairs):** Analyse AQI bar chart for Haridwar vs Delhi (Oct 12–16):
1. Observe the bar chart
2. Fill table — max and min AQI for each city and the day
3. Which day had the biggest difference between Delhi and Haridwar?
4. Delhi has 180 factories, ~half are closed. If each factory employs at least 850 workers, how many workers are currently working?
5. If AQI on Oct 15 and 16 increases by ~80 points, what will Delhi's AQI be? What will the difference with Haridwar be?

### Activity 18: Effect of Smoke on Lungs
**Time:** 20 min | **Materials:** Glass jar, sponge, paper, matchbox

**Reference video:** https://youtu.be/Z-d-HBew4Jk?feature=shared

Attach sponge to jar lid. Burn a piece of paper, place burning paper inside jar. Once smoke fills the jar, close with the sponge-lid. After some time, open and show the sponge.

> [!warning] Safety
> Exercise caution while burning paper and placing inside jar.

**Discussion:**
- What did children observe?
- What does the sponge represent? → Lungs
- How does smoke affect lungs?
- Where have they seen smoke in daily life?
- How can smoke enter lungs? → Smoking, pollution
