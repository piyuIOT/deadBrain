## Activity 1B: Properties of Magnets

**Part A — What does a magnet stick to?**
Students collect nearby objects, test with magnet, fill table:

| # | Objects that stick to magnet | Objects unaffected by magnet |

**Part B — Attraction vs Repulsion**
Test magnet-affected objects, flip them, observe:

| # | Object | Effect (attraction/repulsion) | On flipping (attraction/repulsion) |

**Key conclusion:** Magnet–magnet: if attracts on one side, repels when flipped. Magnet–iron: always attracts regardless of orientation.

## Activity 2: Direction Finding with a Magnet
**Time:** 30 min
**Materials:** Bar magnet, thread, stand, magnetic needle, bowl of water, thermocol ball

- Suspend bar magnet from thread at a fixed point, let it settle
- Observe which direction it aligns to; repeat 2–3 times
- The end pointing toward Earth's geographic North = North pole of magnet

**Conclusion:** A freely suspended magnet always aligns N–S. Earth itself is a giant magnet.

**Alternate experiment:** Float magnet on water (thermocol ball as float)

Students fill table: [#] | [Suspended/floating magnet] | [Direction]

## Activity 3: Introduction to Magnetic Field Lines
**Time:** 30 min
**Materials:** Paper, magnet, iron filings, magnetic compass, pencil/pen

- Place magnet on white paper, slowly sprinkle iron filings around it
- Iron filings arrange in a specific pattern → these lines = magnetic field lines
- Use compass: note compass direction from one pole to other, connect → one magnetic field line
- Direction of field lines: North → South
- Higher density of field lines = stronger magnetic field at that point

## Activity 4: Making a New Magnet
**Time:** 30 min
**Materials:** Bar magnet, iron nails, copper wire, pencil battery

Three methods:
1. **Induction** — Iron nail touching a magnet behaves like a magnet temporarily (temporary magnet)
2. **Friction** — Rubbing iron nail repeatedly in one direction with a magnet → nail becomes permanent magnet
3. **Electromagnet** — Wrap insulated copper wire around iron nail, connect battery → nail becomes electromagnet; remains magnet only while current flows
