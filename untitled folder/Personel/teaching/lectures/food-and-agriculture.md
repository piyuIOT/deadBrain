# Topic: Agriculture & Food (कृषि एवं खाद्यान्न)

**Pages:** 14–33 | **Target:** Class 4–8 | **Language:** Hindi (activity-based)

## Objectives
- Understand food components, their sources and production in plants and animals
- Build understanding of agriculture and balanced diet
- Understand photosynthesis
- Understand crop cycles and production processes
- Understand nutritional deficiency in women/girls due to gender inequality

## Activity 1: Food and Farming — Introduction
**Time:** 20 min

Write "खेती/कृषि" on board → word mapping: students say associated words. Repeat with "खाना/भोजन". Teacher photographs result as baseline for student understanding.

## Activity 2: Journey from Food Made to Plate
**Time:** 45 min

Students list what they eat (breakfast, lunch, dinner) in a table:

| # | Morning breakfast | Lunch/dinner |

Then pick 2–3 food items, trace what ingredients go into making them.

Example: Omelette → egg, onion, red chilli, tomato, green chilli

Trace where those ingredients come from → 3 categories:
- **खेती के माध्यम से** (via farming)
- **पले हुए पशुओं से** (from domesticated animals)
- **बागवानी के माध्यम से** (via gardening/plants)

## Activity 3: Food Items and Plants
**Time:** 15 min

From previously listed food items, pick only those sourced from plants. Fill table:

| # | Food item (plant-sourced) | Plant name | Part of plant used |
|---|--------------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Baingan ka bharta | Brinjal | Fruit |
| 2 | Sarson ka saag | Mustard | Leaves |

Also discuss: roots (potato, onion, carrot, radish), fruits (tomato, brinjal), flower nectar (honey) — all different plant parts provide food.

## Activity 4: Germination Experiment
**Time:** 60 min (spread over multiple days)
**Materials:** Moong or chana seeds, water, bowl, cloth

1. Soak dry seeds in water-filled container for 1 day
2. Drain water, leave seeds in glass, wrap in wet cloth, set aside
3. Observe next day: small white structure emerging = germination
4. If not yet, wash, drain, cover again, wait another day
5. Once germinated, wash and eat — can also be boiled, add spices for snack

**Note:** Sprouted moong/chana are protein-rich.

## Activity 6: Hostel Gardening (Fenugreek)
**Time:** 120 min
**Materials:** Khurpi/spade, measuring tape, compost (or manure), fenugreek seeds

Groups of 5. Each group makes a 2×4 m plot.

**Steps:**
1. Till soil with tools, soften top layer
2. Mix compost if needed
3. Sow seeds, water
4. Water daily; teacher guides
5. Sprouts in 2–3 days, ready to eat in ~15–20 days
6. Do NOT harvest — let one plot go to seed so full seed-to-seed cycle is observed
7. Teacher participates, doesn't just supervise

Students record daily observations:

| # | Date | What did you observe (sprouting, leaves emerging, colour, shape, etc.) |

*Note: Activity adapted from Anand Niketan Vidyalay, Nayi Taleem.*

## Activity 7: Farm Visit
**Time:** 180 min (one session)

Invite a parent who farms. Ideally visit their farm. Run in 3 batches of 2 hrs each (one per class).

Questions to investigate:
- Which crop grows in which season?
- Full farming process: soil preparation → sowing → manure/fertilizer → irrigation → weeding → harvesting → storage
- Pros/cons of manure (खाद) vs chemical fertilizer (उर्वरक)
- What farming tools are used? Name + use
- Organic farming — ask farmer
- Crop rotation (फसल चक्रण) — why alternate crops? (soil nutrient replenishment — use local terms, then explain technically)

## Activity 8: Who is a Farmer? (Gender Activity)
**Time:** 45 min

1. Ask: who is a farmer, what do they do?
2. Each student draws a farmer + lists farm tasks
3. Ask: which tasks are done by men, which by women? Who does more?
4. Match farmer definition to tasks — does that make women farmers too?
5. Did anyone draw a woman as farmer? Why/why not?
6. Discussion: women do equal farm work but are not seen as farmers. This mindset needs to change.

## Activity 9: Testing Food for Nutrients (Starch, Fat, Protein)
**Time:** 90 min
**Materials:** Test tubes, tincture iodine, copper sulfate, caustic soda, water, food samples

**Prepare solutions first:**
- Iodine: few drops tincture iodine in half-filled test tube of water
- Copper sulfate: 2g CuSO₄ in 100 mL water
- Caustic soda: 10g NaOH in 100 mL water

**Test for Starch (मंड):**
- Add 2–3 drops dilute iodine to food sample
- Turns blue/black → starch present

**Test for Protein (प्रोटीन):**
- Grind/paste solid food, add to test tube with 10 drops water, shake
- Add 2 drops CuSO₄ + 10 drops caustic soda, shake, wait
- Turns violet/purple → protein present

**Test for Fat (वसा):**
- Wrap food in paper, crush/rub
- Hold paper to light — oily translucent spot = fat present
- If food has water, dry paper first

**Food samples to test:**

| # | Food | Starch | Fat | Protein |
|---|------|--------|-----|---------|
| 1 | Raw potato | | | |
| 2 | Milk | | | |
| 3 | Peanut | | | |
| 4 | Uncooked rice (ground) | | | |
| 5 | Dry coconut | | | |
| 6 | Uncooked arhar dal | | | |
| 7 | Piece of vegetable | | | |
| 8 | Piece of fruit | | | |

Discussion: Can a food item have more than one nutrient? Did any food have none?
Also mention: vitamins and mineral salts are in food too — all nutrients serve different purposes.

## Activity 10: Assess Own Diet + Balanced Diet
**Time:** 60 min

Groups of 4–5. Each student builds their own diet chart for the past week:

| # | Date | Time | What eaten | Nutrients present |
|---|------|------|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | 24 July | 7:30am breakfast | 1 small cup tea + roti | Carbohydrate, fat |

Video for balanced diet for adolescent girls: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0cBiB6G3EQY
*(Note: Video doesn't mention non-veg — supplement with info on eggs and meat as protein sources)*

After video, compare own diet chart with balanced diet. Group discussion:
1. Which nutrients do I consume least?
2. Which necessary vegetables do I dislike?
3. Modify diet chart to make it balanced
4. During menstruation: iron and folic acid are critical — what do they currently do for this?

## Activity 11: 12 Rotis — Gender Activity
**Time:** 45 min

Hypothetical situation: distribute 12 rotis among 5 family members:
- Labour father, housewife mother, son who works outside, teenage daughter on her period, pregnant daughter-in-law

Then add a guest — who gets rotis now?

Discussion questions:
- Who eats last in a household?
- When something special is cooked, who eats first? (Often the sabzi is left for the mother)
- Is there a difference between what a brother eats vs a sister? Why?
- If a girl eats more vs a boy eating more — do people react differently?
- If a person doesn't get proper nutrition, does it affect them?
- Have you seen malnutrition in women?
- What diseases are common in women with age? (Iron deficiency — facilitator can highlight this)

## Activity 13: Effect of Manure vs Fertilizer on Seeds
**Time:** 60 min (7–10 days)
**Materials:** Moong/chana seeds, cow dung compost, urea fertilizer, 3 glasses labelled A, B, C

- Glass with cow dung compost mixed in soil → seeds
- Glass A: soil + urea → seeds
- Glass B: plain soil → seeds
- Water equally daily for 7–10 days, record growth

Questions:
- Is growth rate the same in all three?
- Which glass has best growth?
- Conclusion?

## Activity 14: Photosynthesis via Video
**Time:** 45 min

Groups of 5 watch video, note important points. Quiz afterwards.

**Quiz questions:**

10. Can non-green plants make food? (Yes — other coloured pigments do the same job)

## Activity 15: Factors Affecting Photosynthesis (Experiments)
**Time:** 60 min (requires advance preparation)

### 1. Effect of Sunlight
- 4–5 days before: put plant in darkness (starves it of starch)
- 4–5 hrs before class: return to sunlight, clip black paper on part of one leaf
- In class: boil leaf in methyl spirit until white (removes chlorophyll)
- Apply iodine solution → black = starch present (photosynthesis happened), no colour change = no photosynthesis (blocked sunlight)
- **Reference video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qr0ARIjqYmk

### 2. Effect of CO₂
- Same dark-starvation preparation as above
- Return to sunlight; seal part of leaf in bottle with KOH solution (KOH absorbs CO₂) using split cork + grease seal
- Boil leaf in methyl spirit → apply iodine → area exposed to KOH shows no starch = CO₂ was removed → no photosynthesis
- **Reference video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1YXMRdPOXw

## Activity 16: Leaf Collection + Plant Nutrition Types
**Time:** 120 min

Students collect leaves from around school/home. Note plant name + any interesting fact. Press and dry in notebook. Glue in notebook, draw outline, write name and fun info.

Identify and find samples of:
- **स्वपोषी** (autotrophs) — make own food (most green plants)
- **परपोषी-परजीवी** (heterotrophic-parasitic)
- **मृतजीवी** (saprotrophic — fungi etc.)
- **सहजीवी** (symbiotic — lichens)
- **कीट-भक्षी** (carnivorous plants)

Student video: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ZAvK94W9bJRI-2naN2xhXwCqaumn4tG/view?usp=drive_link

## Activity 17: Fungi — Bread Mould Experiment
**Time:** 60 min
**Materials:** Magnifying lens/microscope, bread, water

- 2–3 days before class: soak bread in water, keep in warm humid place
  - Monsoon: outdoors; Summer: near water tap; Winter: in glass container in sun, lightly sprinkled with water
- Observe until fluffy/cotton-like structures appear
- Observe under magnifying glass/microscope
- Record observations in notebook

**Key concept — Fungi (कवक/फंजाई):**
- Saprotrophic nutrition: secrete digestive juices on dead/decaying matter, absorb dissolved nutrients
- No chlorophyll
- Grow on pickles, leather, cloth, other organic matter
- Best growth conditions: warm and moist → monsoon season

**Algae (शैवाल):** green floating matter on stagnant water or on stones. When they multiply rapidly in a pond, they consume oxygen → kills aquatic life.

**Lichen (लाइकेन):** symbiotic relationship between fungi and algae. Algae provides chlorophyll → photosynthesis; Fungi provides water, shelter, nutrients. Found on trees or in extreme humidity/dryness.
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TKOv2XWxoeY
