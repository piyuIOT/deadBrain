# Light (प्रकाश क्या है?)

**Objective:** Understand light and its properties through simple experiments. Study lenses and mirrors and their daily-life use. Understand the human eye and Braille script (brief).

## Activity 1 — How Light Travels (45 min)

**Materials:** Flexible pipe, candle/bulb, transparent glass with clean water

1. Ask children: how does light travel from a source?
2. Look through a straight flexible pipe at a light source — visible.
3. Bend the pipe slightly — light source disappears.
4. Discussion questions:
   - Does light travel in a straight line?
   - Can a light ray be bent?

**Extension:** Shine a laser through a clear glass of water — beam invisible. Add mud/dust particles — beam becomes visible as a straight line. Real-life examples: sunbeam through a small hole in a dark room, streetlight in fog.

## Activity 2 — Classification of Objects by Light Interaction (45 min)

**Materials:** Stones, paper, glass, polythene, wood, cloth, cardboard, butter paper

Hold each object in front of a light source. Classify:

| Category | Hindi | Behaviour |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Opaque (अपारदर्शी) | blocks light completely | stone, wood, cardboard |
| Translucent (धुंधली/पारभासी) | allows partial light | butter paper, thin cloth |
| Transparent (पारदर्शी) | allows full light | glass, polythene |

Students make their own classification table from objects collected around them.

## Activity 3 — Reflection of Light (90 min)

**Materials:** Plane, convex, concave mirrors; spoon, bowl, car side mirror

### (A) Plane Mirror
- Place a burning candle in front of a plane mirror.
- Observe: image properties, distance, size.
- Can the image be projected on a screen? No → **virtual image**.

**Laws of Reflection:**
1. Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
2. Incident ray, normal at point of incidence, and reflected ray — all lie in the same plane.

### (B) Spherical Mirrors

| Type | Hindi | Surface | Example |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| Concave (अवतल) | अवतल दर्पण | Shiny on inner curved side | Torch reflector, solar cooker, bowl inside |
| Convex (उत्तल) | उत्तल दर्पण | Shiny on outer curved side | Car side mirror, road-bend mirror |

**Spoon experiment:** Look at inside of spoon (concave) → inverted image. Flip spoon (convex) → upright image. Move spoon away → image changes.

**Concave + sunlight:** Focus sunlight to a point on paper using concave mirror → paper can catch fire. Real-life: solar cooker.

**Image observation table (concave/convex):** Record object distance, image distance, image size (smaller/larger), upright/inverted, real/virtual.

## Activity 4 — Lenses and Light (75 min)

**Materials:** Concave lens, convex lens, magnifying lens, paper, candle

Two types of lenses:
- **Concave lens (अवतल लेंस)** — thinner at centre
- **Convex lens (उत्तल लेंस)** — thicker at centre

**Convex lens experiment:** Place lit candle, convex lens, and wall in a straight line (lens between candle and wall). Get image on wall. Vary distances and record:

| Object distance from lens | Image distance from lens | Image size (smaller/larger) | Upright/Inverted | Real/Virtual |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | |

**More experiments:**
- Convex lens + sunlight → focus to a point on paper → paper burns. Real use: fire lighting.
- Convex lens as magnifier → newspaper text appears larger.

## Activity 6 — Extra Experiments (135 min total)

### A. Prism & Dispersion of White Light (20 min)
White light (sunlight) through a glass prism splits into 7 colours — **VIBGYOR**:
Violet · Indigo · Blue · Green · Yellow · Orange · Red

- Violet bends the most, Red bends the least.
- This phenomenon = **Dispersion (विक्षेपण)**
- Natural prism example: **Rainbow** — water droplets act as tiny prisms.
- Reference: *Prism & dispersion of light (Hindi) — Khan Academy*

### B. Newton's Colour Disc / Rang Birangi Chakri (20 min)
- Paint 7 VIBGYOR segments on a cardboard disc.
- Spin it fast → disc appears **white**.
- Called **Newton's Disc** — proves white light = combination of 7 colours.

### C. Periscope (20 min)
- Built using **2 plane mirrors** at 45°.
- Works on multiple reflection principle.
- Real use: **submarines** — view surface activity while submerged.

### D. Kaleidoscope / Bahuroopdarshak (20 min)
- Works on **multiple image** principle.
- 2+ plane mirrors at an angle → multiple reflections of coloured beads.
- Experiment: place a coin between 2 mirrors at 90° → count images. Change angle → image count changes.
- Formula: number of images = $\frac{360°}{\theta} - 1$

### E. Pinhole Camera / Soochi Chidra Camera (20 min)
- Light travels in straight lines → image formed through small hole is **inverted**.
- Make with cardboard box, butter paper screen, pin hole.
- Ask: what kind of image is seen?

### F. Bird in Cage — Persistence of Vision (20 min)
- Bird and cage drawn on opposite sides of a card. Spin fast → bird appears inside cage.
- Demonstrates **persistence of vision**: retina retains an image for **1/16 second** after stimulus is removed.
- Application: **television, cinema** — rapid sequence of still frames creates illusion of motion.

### G. Water Bowl Rainbow (15 min)
- Mirror placed in a bowl of water at an angle + sunlight → **rainbow on wall**.
- Water + mirror together act as a prism → dispersion of white light into 7 colours.
