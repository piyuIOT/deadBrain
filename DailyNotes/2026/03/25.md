# Notes
## Piezo vs Accelerometer — BCM Low-Frequency Reading

BCM driven at various sine-wave frequencies; output read via piezo and accelerometer.

**Root cause:** Piezo voltage $\propto$ displacement. Accelerometer measures acceleration = second derivative of displacement.

$$x(t) = A \sin(\omega t + \phi)$$
$$v(t) = A\omega \cos(\omega t + \phi)$$
$$a(t) = -A\omega^2 \sin(\omega t + \phi)$$

The $\omega^2$ term means acceleration scales with frequency squared. At low $\omega$, acceleration is small even if displacement $A$ is unchanged.

- To compare accelerometer output across frequencies: divide by $\omega^2$
- To recover displacement from accelerometer: double-integrate, or divide frequency-domain amplitude by $\omega^2$
- Piezo is the better sensor for low-frequency BCM characterisation

---

# Conclusion
> Piezo (displacement-proportional) is the right sensor for low-freq BCM work. Accelerometer readings need $\omega^2$ correction to be meaningful across frequencies.
