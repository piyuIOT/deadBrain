**Instead of full STT on mcu:**  
Cant we try Speech to phoneme detection on mcu  
since speech to text try to learn thousands of words,  
speech to phoneme will only try to learn a few phoneme (which should make it fairly small model)  
these set of phoneme, together will take a lot less space (easy on bluetooth), almost similar to text  
and then on the backend, we send this phoneme file and use a model that is trained to convert phoneme to text?

got this from the idea that  
wakeword and intent engine kinda use phoneme matching  
which means phoneme can easily be recognised on edge!

Paper on the same: [Real-time low-resource phoneme recognition on edge devices](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.13997)

People use such speech to phoneme models to do mouth animation from voice:  
- [https://www.edgeimpulse.com/blog/bringing-animation-to-life-with-phoneme-classifier-and-articulatory-speech-synthesis-2/](https://www.edgeimpulse.com/blog/bringing-animation-to-life-with-phoneme-classifier-and-articulatory-speech-synthesis-2/)
- [https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1910.08685](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1910.08685)

The issue that I see is:  
Doing this from a Air conduction mic shouldnt be hard  
but from a bone conduction mic, we are already loosing a lot of frequency band  
a lot of phoneme lives in those frequencies and they probably won't be detected well 
you can understand a word because you know a word that sound like that  
but a phoneme has to sound very close to what it is
